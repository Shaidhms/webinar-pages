🎩 The Six Thinking Hats

⚪

White Hat

Facts & Data

Focus on verified facts and information. What do we know for certain? What data do we need? Where are the gaps in our knowledge?

🔴

Red Hat

Emotions & Intuition

Express feelings, hunches, and gut reactions. What is your emotional response? What does your intuition tell you?

⚫

Black Hat

Critical Thinking

Identify risks, problems, and potential downsides. What could go wrong? What are the challenges and obstacles?

🟡

Yellow Hat

Optimism & Benefits

Focus on positive aspects, benefits, and opportunities. What are the advantages? Why might this work?

🟢

Green Hat

Creativity & Ideas

Generate new ideas, alternatives, and creative solutions. Think outside the box and explore possibilities.

🔵

Blue Hat

Process Control

Manage the thinking process. Set objectives, control the discussion flow, and ensure proper hat sequencing.

🔄 Implementation Process

1. Set the Objective (Blue Hat)

Define what you want to achieve. Are you making a decision, solving a problem, or generating ideas?

🎯
📊

2. Gather Facts (White Hat)

Collect and share all relevant data and information. Identify what you know and what's missing.

3. Express Feelings (Red Hat)

Allow everyone to share their gut reactions and emotional responses without justification.

❤️
💡

4. Generate Ideas (Green Hat)

Brainstorm creative solutions and alternatives without immediate judgment.

5. Explore Benefits (Yellow Hat)

Focus on the positive aspects and potential benefits of each option.

☀️
⚠️

6. Identify Risks (Black Hat)

Examine potential problems, challenges, and risks associated with each option.

7. Conclude (Blue Hat)

Summarize insights, make decisions, and plan next steps based on the structured thinking process.
